## xT CDx

- **Indication / primary use case:** FDA-approved, 648-gene next-generation sequencing (NGS) tissue test for comprehensive molecular profiling of solid malignant tumors. Uses a tumor + normal matched approach to identify somatic variants, insertions/deletions, and microsatellite instability (MSI). Intended as a companion diagnostic (CDx) for select targeted therapies and as broad tumor mutation profiling per professional oncology guidelines.
- **Strongest clinical fit:** Thoracic Oncology (NSCLC biomarker workup), GI Oncology (CRC with CDx claims), Breast Oncology (baseline tissue profiling in complex or treatment-naïve cases), GU Oncology (solid tumor profiling when tissue is available).
- **Sample type required:** Formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded (FFPE) tumor tissue plus matched normal specimen (blood or saliva).
- **Turnaround time:** 14 calendar days from sample receipt to report delivery.
- **Key differentiator vs. other Tempus products:** Broadest DNA-level tissue panel with FDA-approved CDx status and tumor-normal matching for higher-confidence somatic variant calls; foundation for adding RNA (xR) or liquid (xF+) when tissue is sufficient.

## xR

- **Indication / primary use case:** Whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing panel for solid tumors and hematologic malignancies. Detects clinically relevant gene fusions across 100+ targeted genes and altered splicing events (e.g., MET exon 14 skipping, EGFRvIII). Complements DNA profiling when fusions, splice variants, or transcriptional signatures are clinically relevant.
- **Strongest clinical fit:** Thoracic Oncology (ALK, ROS1, RET, MET exon 14 fusions in NSCLC), GI Oncology (fusion-driven subtypes), Hematologic Oncology (fusion detection in lymphoid and myeloid disease), Breast Oncology (incremental RNA signal when DNA alone may miss actionable events).
- **Sample type required:** FFPE tissue or fresh/frozen tissue with sufficient RNA yield; typically ordered with or following xT CDx tissue submission.
- **Turnaround time:** 12 calendar days from sample receipt to report delivery (when run concurrently with xT CDx tissue workflow).
- **Key differentiator vs. other Tempus products:** RNA-level fusion and splice-event detection that DNA-only panels cannot reliably capture; strongest additive value when the clinical question involves gene rearrangements or transcriptomic biomarkers.

## xF+

- **Indication / primary use case:** 523-gene circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) liquid biopsy panel covering SNVs, indels, copy number gains, and gene rearrangements. Used when tissue is insufficient, unavailable, or when repeat non-invasive monitoring is needed. Identifies variants potentially associated with clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) for interpretive context.
- **Strongest clinical fit:** Breast Oncology (metastatic disease, therapy changes, resistance monitoring), Thoracic Oncology (time-sensitive first-line decisions, tissue insufficiency), GU Oncology (mCRPC and bladder cancer when biopsy is deferred), GI Oncology (progression and later-line profiling when re-biopsy is difficult).
- **Sample type required:** Peripheral blood (plasma ctDNA); no tissue required.
- **Turnaround time:** 7 calendar days from sample receipt to report delivery.
- **Key differentiator vs. other Tempus products:** Fastest non-invasive path to broad genomic results; ideal fallback when tissue quantity or timing blocks xT CDx, and for serial monitoring without repeat biopsy.

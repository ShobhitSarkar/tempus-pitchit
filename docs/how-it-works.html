How this app works

A plain-language tour of the whole thing: one program that both shows the web page and answers its questions, how the data flows, and where the "AI" is currently faked.

The one-sentence version: A single Python program (FastAPI) does two jobs at once — it hands your browser the web page, and it answers the small data questions that page asks. There is no separate server and no database. The AI parts make real calls to OpenAI when a key is configured, and fall back to a built-in stand-in when it isn't.

3. What happens when you load the page

  1. Browser asks for the page. FastAPI hands back the built React app from dist/.
  2. The app asks "who should I call?" It calls GET /api/providers/ranked.
  3. The app scores & sorts all 7 providers on the spot (see section 5) and returns them best-first.
  4. The app auto-picks #1 and quietly asks POST /api/providers/{id}/generate for that doctor's call brief, so the prep panel is never empty.

Everything you see — the queue, the score bars, the product fit, the talking points — comes from those two API calls.

5. How the ranking score is calculated

Each provider gets three sub-scores from 0–100, and the final score is a weighted blend of them. The weights are what the sliders change.

score = Volume × wV + Product fit × wF + Engagement × wE
Default weights: Volume 20%, Product fit 40%, Engagement 40% (they always add up to 100%).
Sub-scoreWhere it comes from
VolumeHow many addressable patients per month, scaled against the busiest provider (busiest = 100).
Product fitThe best of the three product-fit numbers for that doctor's specialty. The winner gets the "Best fit" badge.
EngagementThe CRM "warmth" score judged from their notes by the AI (see section 6).
The one special case — Dr. Lena Vasquez (P007): she has no CRM notes, so there's no Engagement score to use. For her, the app drops that term and re-splits the remaining weight between Volume and Product fit. That's why she shows a "No CRM history" badge and only two score bars.

6. Where the "AI" is

There are two AI touch-points. Each one makes a real OpenAI API call when the app has a key (OPENAI_API_KEY), and quietly falls back to a built-in stand-in when it doesn't — so the demo always runs, even with no key.

SpotWith a key real AIWithout a key fallback
Scoring CRM notes
the "warmth" number		 reads each doctor's notes and judges how ready-to-buy they are, plus pulls out their top objection & interest. a stable made-up score per provider.
Generating the brief
pitch & talking points		 writes a tailored pitch and Q&A for that specific doctor, grounded in the product facts. fills a fixed sentence template with real product facts.
How the switch works: if OPENAI_API_KEY is set, real calls run (model gpt-4o-mini by default). If it's missing — or a call errors — the app uses the fallback instead of failing. Everything around the AI (data cleaning, scoring math, ranking, sliders, note-saving, the whole UI) is the same either way.
